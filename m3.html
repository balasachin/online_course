Course Description:


Permutations and combinations, the various ways in which objects from a set may be selected, generally without replacement, to form subsets. ... This selection of subsets is called a permutation when the order of selection is a factor, a combination when order is not a factor. permutations and combination.a permutation of a set is, loosely speaking, an arrangement of its members into a sequence or linear order, or if the set is already ordered, a rearrangement of its elements. The word "permutation" also refers to the act or process of changing the linear order of an ordered set.

Prerequisites:


Logical thinking skills
